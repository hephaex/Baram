# [News Today] President returns to Cheong Wa Dae

**KBS** | 2025-12-30 00:00 | 

---

[LEAD]President Lee Jae Myung has begun work at Cheong Wa Dae, ending the Yongsan era of the presidential office.It marks the president&#x27;s return to the former compound after 1,330 days.President Lee described the move as a symbolic reset, saying it marked the end of a period marred by constitutional disruption.[REPORT]A flag bearing the president&#x27;s symbol, the phoenix, is raised, declaring the beginning of another Cheong Wa Dae era.President Lee Jae Myung went to work, wearing a three-color striped tie, which is said to symbolize unity.This marks a return to Cheong Wa Dae in 1,330 days after former President Moon Jae-in finished his term and left office.Lee received a briefing on state affairs during a tea meeting with aides, which lasted for more than an hour.As his first official engagement since returning to Cheong Wa Dae, he visited the National Crisis Management Center, the underground bunker at Cheong Wa Dae, and inspected the security and disaster system.Kang Yu-jung / Cheong Wa Dae SpokespersonAs the president in charge of ensuring public safety, it was a move to check how well the system is being maintained and operated.Instead of the main building of Cheong Wa Dae, the president decided to set up his main office in the Yeomin 1-gwan building used by his three key senior aides.Lee Jae Myung / PresidentIt marks a milestone symbolizing the end of the Yongsan era, tainted by the erosion of the constitutional order, and the restoration of popular sovereignty and democratic norms.The president also made a surprise visit to Chunchugwan, which houses the press briefing room and an area for reporters.This is seen as a move to dispel concerns about a possible lack of communication, which could result from the presidential compound&#x27;s structural exclusivity.Ending the Yongsan era of the presidential office, Lee finished his move to Cheong Wa Dae with plans to spur the economy&#x27;s potential growth and speed up his
