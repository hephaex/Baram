# Seoul shares up late Mon. morning on chip rally

**연합뉴스** | 2025-12-29 00:00 | 

---

stocks-morning Seoul shares up late Mon. morning on chip rally SEOUL, Dec. 29 (Yonhap) -- South Korean stocks traded markedly higher late Monday morning, led by semiconductor shares after the investment warning designation on SK hynix was lifted. The benchmark Korea Composite Stock Price Index (KOSPI) gained 53.29 points, or 1.29 percent, to 4,182.97 as of 11:20 a.m. The index opened higher and maintained the momentum on buying by retail and foreign investors.

Chip giant SK hynix jumped 4.51 percent after the bourse operator, the Korea Exchange (KRX), lifted its investment warning designation as of Monday. The warning had been in place for 10 trading sessions from Dec. 11 following recent sharp gains. Market bellwether Samsung Electronics advanced 1.11 percent. Defense giant Hanwha Aerospace spiked 8.28 percent, and nuclear power plant builder Doosan Enerbility added 2.58 percent. Carmakers gathered ground. Top automaker Hyundai Motor gained 1.92 percent, and its sister affiliate Kia increased 0.67 percent. But leading battery maker LG Energy Solution slid 1.69 percent on news that it has canceled the supply deal worth 3.9 trillion won signed with U.S.-based battery pack producer Freudenberg Battery Power System (FBPS) LLC. Leading shipbuilder HD Hyundai Heavy grew 1.56 percent, while its rival Hanwha Ocean edged up 0.09 percent. Internet portal operator Naver soared 3.46 percent, and No. 1 steelmaker POSCO rose 1.3 percent. The local currency was trading at 1,433.95 won against the U.S. dollar at 11:20 a.m., up 6.35 won from the previous session. graceoh@yna.co.kr(END)
