# Loans from private money lenders gain slightly in 1st half

**연합뉴스** | 2025-12-30 00:00 | 

---

private lenders-loan tally Loans from private money lenders gain slightly in 1st half SEOUL, Dec. 30 (Yonhap) -- Loans extended by private money lenders in South Korea slightly increased in the first half of the year from the previous six months, data showed Tuesday. As of end-June, outstanding loans from private money lenders stood at 12.45 trillion won (US$8.69 billion), up 121 billion won, or 1 percent, from 12.33 trillion won tallied at the end of last year, according to the data from the Financial Supervisory Service (FSS). The number of people who borrowed money from private lenders also rose 1.3 percent to some 717,000 from about 708,000 over the cited period. The average amount of money owed by individual borrowers came to 17.37 million won as of end-June, down from 17.42 million won six months earlier, according to the FSS. Meanwhile, the financial watchdog said the number of registered private money lenders came to 8,203 at end-June, up from 8,182 tallied six months before.
